Pocket Monster — Swirly is alive.
The creature is a fully hand-drawn SVG — round blue body with that radial gradient sheen, stubby arms, two little round feet with three toe bumps each, a curled tail that gently sways on its own CSS animation, big circular eyes with gradient irises, white catch-light circles, and a little smile. It blinks at random intervals. The lighter belly oval sits center-chest, and that's where the magic lives.
The belly canvas IS the synth visualizer — a live canvas drawn directly over the belly ellipse, reacting to audio energy in real time:

Sine — ecto green: five concentric rings that wobble and breathe, smooth and organic, a center glowing dot that pulses with volume
Square — orange: rotating nested squares that snap angularly, with pixel-spark dots firing off the edges
Sawtooth — soul purple: three asymmetric pinwheel arms sweeping in a spiral with trailing dot particles
Triangle — gold: six-pointed star pattern with four nested rings rotating in alternating directions

When you play a note the swirl accelerates and brightens. When you stop it slowly drifts back down.
Click the creature body to cycle through wave shapes. The whole interface color-shifts — header, waveform, display panel, creature glow, atmosphere bubbles. Keys 1 2 3 4 also switch waves instantly.
The atmosphere is tiny blue bubbles rising slowly upward with little highlight dots inside them like real bubbles. They wobble side to side.
